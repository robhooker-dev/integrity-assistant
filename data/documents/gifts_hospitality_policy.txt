GIFTS, GRATUITIES AND HOSPITALITY POLICY
Version: 2.4 | Review Date: January 2026 | Owner: Professional Standards Department

[DEMO DOCUMENT - Based on publicly available national guidance and force policies]

1. PURPOSE

1.1 This policy governs the acceptance, refusal, and declaration of gifts, gratuities and hospitality offered to officers and staff in connection with their police duties.

1.2 The integrity of the force depends on public confidence that its officers and staff cannot be influenced by the receipt of gifts or hospitality. This policy exists to protect both the force and individual officers from real or perceived corruption.

2. GENERAL PRINCIPLE

2.1 The default position is that gifts, gratuities and hospitality offered in connection with police duties should not be accepted.

2.2 The Standards of Professional Behaviour require officers and staff to not accept gifts or gratuities that could compromise their impartiality or integrity, or that of the force.

2.3 When in doubt about whether something should be accepted or declared, officers and staff should err on the side of caution and declare it.

3. CATEGORIES

3.1 GIFTS
A gift is any item of monetary or perceived value offered to an officer or member of staff. This includes physical goods, vouchers, digital goods, and services provided free of charge.

3.2 GRATUITIES
A gratuity is a payment or reward beyond what is due. This includes cash payments, tips, or anything with a cash value offered as a reward for service.

3.3 HOSPITALITY
Hospitality includes the provision of food, drink, accommodation, entertainment, travel, or tickets to events where these are provided free of charge or at a discounted rate.

4. WHAT MUST BE DECLARED

4.1 All officers and staff must declare the following, regardless of whether they are accepted or refused:

(a) Any gift with an estimated value over £10
(b) Any offer of hospitality in connection with police duties
(c) Any repeated offers of lower value gifts from the same source
(d) Any gift or hospitality offered in circumstances which could create a perception of impropriety
(e) Any gift or hospitality offered by a person or organisation who has, or is seeking, a business relationship with the force
(f) Any gift or hospitality offered by a person known to be the subject of a police investigation

4.2 Declarations must be made to the officer's line manager and recorded on the force gifts and hospitality register within 5 working days of the offer being made.

5. WHAT MAY BE ACCEPTED WITHOUT DECLARATION

5.1 The following may generally be accepted without formal declaration:
(a) Promotional items of negligible value (e.g. pens, notepads) with an estimated value under £5
(b) Refreshments of a minor nature provided during the course of a meeting (e.g. tea, coffee, biscuits)
(c) Working meals provided as part of a legitimate professional meeting where the value is reasonable and proportionate

5.2 Even in the above circumstances, officers should exercise judgment. If there is any doubt, a declaration should be made.

6. WHAT MUST BE REFUSED

6.1 The following must always be refused:
(a) Cash or cash equivalents (vouchers, gift cards) of any value
(b) Any gift or hospitality from a person currently under investigation by the force
(c) Any gift or hospitality that could create an obligation or perception of obligation
(d) Any gift or hospitality offered in circumstances suggesting an attempt to influence a policing decision
(e) Gifts or hospitality of a sexual nature

7. DECLARATION PROCESS

7.1 Declarations are made to the officer's direct line manager.

7.2 The line manager records the declaration on the force gifts and hospitality register.

7.3 The line manager determines whether the gift or hospitality was appropriately refused or may be accepted, and records the outcome.

7.4 Where there is doubt about the appropriate outcome, the matter should be referred to the Professional Standards Department.

7.5 The gifts and hospitality register is subject to periodic audit by the PSD.

8. CORPORATE GIFTS

8.1 Gifts and hospitality offered to the force as a corporate body rather than to individuals should be referred to the officer's line manager and the CCU/PSD for guidance.

8.2 Where corporate gifts are accepted on behalf of the force, they are recorded as force property.

9. SEASONAL GIFTS

9.1 Seasonal periods (Christmas, Eid, Diwali etc.) may generate increased offers of gifts and hospitality. The same rules apply at all times of year.

9.2 Gifts of food offered to a team or department collectively (e.g. chocolates, biscuits) may generally be accepted where they are shared among the team and have a nominal individual value. They should still be declared to the line manager.

10. CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE

10.1 Failure to declare gifts or hospitality, or acceptance of gifts or hospitality in breach of this policy, may constitute misconduct or gross misconduct.

10.2 Acceptance of gifts or hospitality that could indicate corruption may be referred to the Counter Corruption Unit and may result in criminal investigation.
