NOTIFIABLE ASSOCIATIONS POLICY
Version: 3.1 | Review Date: April 2026 | Owner: Professional Standards Department

[DEMO DOCUMENT - Based on publicly available national guidance and force policies]

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 This policy sets out the obligations on all police officers, police staff, special constables and volunteers to declare associations that may affect their integrity or the integrity of the force.

1.2 This policy applies to all personnel from the point of appointment and throughout their service. The duty to declare is ongoing and does not diminish with length of service.

1.3 This policy is underpinned by the College of Policing Code of Ethics, the Standards of Professional Behaviour, and the Authorised Professional Practice (APP) for Vetting.

2. DEFINITION OF A NOTIFIABLE ASSOCIATION

2.1 A notifiable association (NIA) exists where a member of the force knows, considers, or suspects that they have a personal association with an individual, group, or organisation which is, or may be, notifiable.

2.2 An association is notifiable where the person or group falls into one or more of the following categories:

(a) Persons known to be charged with a criminal offence and the subject of a current prosecution
(b) Persons known to have unspent criminal convictions
(c) Persons known to be the subject of a current police investigation
(d) Persons known to be or suspected of being involved in serious organised crime
(e) Persons known to be or suspected of being involved in drug supply or use
(f) Persons known to be or suspected of being involved in domestic extremism or terrorism
(g) Persons who have been dismissed from any police force or law enforcement agency
(h) Persons who are or have been the subject of counter-corruption intelligence
(i) Members of groups or organisations whose aims or activities are incompatible with the Standards of Professional Behaviour

2.3 There is no definitive list of associations that will be deemed notifiable. The obligation is on the individual to consider whether an association could compromise their integrity or the integrity of the force.

3. THE DUTY TO DECLARE

3.1 All personnel have a personal and continuing duty to declare any association that falls or may fall within the criteria set out in Section 2.

3.2 Declarations must be made to the Counter Corruption Unit (CCU) / Anti-Corruption Unit (ACU) who are responsible for recording and assessing the association.

3.3 The duty to declare applies regardless of:
- Whether the association was declared at recruitment or vetting
- Whether the association has been previously assessed
- The length of service of the individual
- The rank or grade of the individual

3.4 Where circumstances change in relation to a previously declared association, the change must be reported immediately.

3.5 The onus is on the individual to report. Uncertainty about whether something is notifiable should result in a declaration, not inaction. The guiding principle is: if in doubt, declare.

4. WHAT IS NOT AUTOMATICALLY A NOTIFIABLE ASSOCIATION

4.1 Not all associations with persons who have come to the attention of police will be notifiable. Examples of associations which may not be notifiable include:

(a) Professional contacts made in the course of police duty
(b) Brief or superficial contact with members of the public
(c) Family members where there is no ongoing contact and the relationship is estranged

4.2 However, even in these circumstances, if there is any doubt, a declaration should be made.

5. THE DECLARATION PROCESS

5.1 Declarations should be made as soon as the officer or staff member becomes aware of the potential notifiable association.

5.2 Declarations are made to the CCU/ACU via the force's designated reporting mechanism.

5.3 Upon receipt of a declaration, the CCU/ACU will:
(a) Acknowledge receipt of the declaration
(b) Conduct an intelligence assessment of the association
(c) Determine whether continued association is appropriate
(d) Record the outcome on the individual's vetting file
(e) Communicate the outcome to the individual and their line manager

5.4 The individual will be informed of whether:
(a) The association is assessed as posing no integrity risk — no further action required
(b) The association requires ongoing monitoring
(c) The association is incompatible with continued employment or deployment in sensitive roles
(d) The individual should cease the association

6. SUPERVISOR RESPONSIBILITIES

6.1 Supervisors have a specific responsibility to:
(a) Ensure officers and staff under their command are aware of this policy
(b) Actively identify and act on information that may indicate an undeclared notifiable association
(c) Report any undeclared or suspected notifiable associations to the CCU/ACU
(d) Not assess or adjudicate on potential NIAs themselves — this is the sole responsibility of the CCU/ACU

6.2 Where a supervisor identifies a potential NIA or receives information suggesting one exists, they must refer the matter to the CCU/ACU without delay.

6.3 Supervisors must not make commitments to officers or staff about the likely outcome of a notifiable association assessment. Outcomes are determined solely by the CCU/ACU following intelligence assessment.

7. FAMILY AND HOUSEHOLD MEMBERS

7.1 Officers and staff are not responsible for the conduct of family members. However, where a family member falls within the criteria in Section 2, this must be declared.

7.2 Particular consideration must be given to household members given the access they may have to force-related information, equipment, or the officer's working environment.

7.3 The force recognises the sensitive nature of disclosures involving family members and will handle all such declarations with appropriate discretion.

8. CONSEQUENCES OF NON-DECLARATION

8.1 Failure to declare a notifiable association is a serious breach of the Standards of Professional Behaviour and may constitute misconduct or gross misconduct.

8.2 The act of non-disclosure may be treated more seriously than the underlying association itself.

8.3 Non-declaration may also result in:
(a) Withdrawal of vetting clearance
(b) Restricted deployment
(c) Referral to the IOPC
(d) Criminal proceedings where appropriate

9. CONFIDENTIALITY AND WELFARE

9.1 All declarations will be handled with discretion and stored securely in accordance with data protection legislation.

9.2 Information about declarations will only be shared on a need-to-know basis.

9.3 Officers and staff who are unsure whether to declare an association can seek confidential advice from their staff association or trade union representative.

9.4 The force's confidential reporting mechanism is also available for officers or staff who wish to report concerns about others anonymously.
