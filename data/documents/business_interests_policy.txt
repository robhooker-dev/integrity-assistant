BUSINESS INTERESTS AND SECONDARY EMPLOYMENT POLICY
Version: 1.8 | Review Date: March 2026 | Owner: Professional Standards Department

[DEMO DOCUMENT - Based on publicly available national guidance and force policies]

1. PURPOSE

1.1 This policy governs the declaration and approval of business interests and secondary employment held by officers and staff in addition to their role with the force.

1.2 The force recognises that officers and staff may have legitimate interests outside of policing. This policy ensures that those interests do not conflict with their policing duties or undermine public confidence.

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 BUSINESS INTEREST
Any direct or indirect financial interest in a business, company, or commercial enterprise. This includes shares (above 1% of a listed company), directorships, partnerships, sole trader activities, and land or property interests used for commercial purposes.

2.2 SECONDARY EMPLOYMENT
Any paid or unpaid work carried out for another employer or organisation in addition to the officer's or staff member's police role. This includes self-employment.

3. THE DUTY TO DECLARE

3.1 All officers and staff must declare to the force:
(a) Any business interest held by them or a member of their household
(b) Any secondary employment, whether paid or unpaid
(c) Any change to a previously declared business interest or secondary employment

3.2 Declarations must be made before commencing any new business interest or secondary employment.

3.3 Existing interests must be reviewed and re-declared annually.

4. APPROVAL PROCESS

4.1 Business interests and secondary employment require force approval before commencement.

4.2 Approval will not normally be granted where the business interest or secondary employment:
(a) Would bring the force into disrepute
(b) Would create a conflict of interest with policing duties
(c) Would involve the use of police skills, powers, or information in a private capacity
(d) Would involve working for a person or organisation known to be involved in criminal activity
(e) Would impair the officer's or staff member's ability to perform their police role
(f) Would involve work in licensed premises (subject to specific restrictions)
(g) Would create a financial relationship with a person under police investigation

4.3 Officers and staff must not commence business interests or secondary employment pending approval.

5. SPECIFIC RESTRICTIONS

5.1 PRIVATE INVESTIGATION
Officers and staff may not work as private investigators, either directly or through a company. This restriction exists due to the direct conflict with policing functions and the risk of misuse of police information.

5.2 SECURITY INDUSTRY
Work in the private security industry requires careful assessment. Officers wishing to work in security roles must obtain specific approval and must not use their status as a police officer in connection with their security work.

5.3 LICENSED PREMISES
Officers and staff must not hold a financial interest in or work at licensed premises where this would create a conflict of interest with their policing role. This is assessed on a case by case basis.

5.4 LEGAL SERVICES
Officers and staff may not work as legal representatives in cases involving the force.

6. REVIEW AND WITHDRAWAL OF APPROVAL

6.1 Approval may be reviewed at any time where circumstances change.

6.2 Approval may be withdrawn where:
(a) The nature of the business interest or employment changes
(b) A conflict of interest arises
(c) The individual's circumstances change
(d) The force's operational requirements change

7. HOUSEHOLD MEMBERS

7.1 Business interests held by household members must also be declared where those interests could create a conflict of interest or integrity concern.

7.2 Officers and staff are not required to control the business interests of household members, but must be transparent about their existence.

8. CONSEQUENCES OF NON-COMPLIANCE

8.1 Failure to declare or obtain approval for business interests or secondary employment may constitute misconduct.

8.2 Where undeclared interests are identified retrospectively, the force will assess whether disciplinary action is appropriate.
